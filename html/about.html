About Me

A simple bio with the big pieces of my story.

I am Josef Johnsen, a BYU student studying French and preparing for a future that blends people, leadership, and meaningful work. I care a lot about growth, service, and showing up well for the teams and communities around me.

My Story

I grew up in Utah and built a lot of my early identity through school, athletics, and service. At Skyline High School I stayed involved in leadership, academics, and sports, which taught me how to work hard, stay dependable, and help bring people together.

One of the biggest shaping experiences in my life was serving a two-year mission in Côte d'Ivoire. Living in different communities, teaching in French, and working closely with people from different backgrounds stretched me in a really good way. That experience strengthened my confidence, communication, and perspective.

Now I am at Brigham Young University studying French and building toward business-focused opportunities. I am especially interested in work where leadership, service, and real human connection matter just as much as technical skill.
